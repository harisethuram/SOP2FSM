SOP: Transportation of Temperature-Sensitive Medicinal Products for Clinical Trials

Step 1: Confirm staff training.
  Check: Has the staff member who will collect and transport the temperature-sensitive medicinal product (MP) received study-specific training, and is this recorded in the study training log?
  - If yes: Go to Step 2.
  - If no: Complete study-specific training and record it in the training log before handling any MPs.

Step 2: Verify MP suitability.
  Check: Has the Pharmacy Clinical Trials Manager confirmed that the temperature-sensitive MP received for the trial is suitable for use?
  - If yes: Go to Step 3.
  - If no: The Pharmacy Clinical Trials Manager must verify suitability and ensure suitable packaging and temperature monitoring equipment (e.g. thermometers) are available.

Step 3: Inspect the MP before transport.
  Check: Does the MP appear undamaged?
  - If yes: Go to Step 4.
  - If no (MP appears damaged): Do NOT administer the MP. Return it unused to the Clinical Trials Pharmacy Department, who will advise on next steps. END.

Step 4: Confirm the MP is in its original packaging.
  Check: Is the medicinal product still in the manufacturer's original, sealed packaging?
  - If yes: Go to Step 4a.
  - If no: Do not remove the MP from its original packaging. If the original packaging is missing or compromised, contact the Clinical Trials Pharmacy Department for guidance before proceeding.

Step 4a: Place the MP in a suitable transport container.
  Check: Has the MP (in its original packaging) been placed in an appropriate cool pack/box that is NOT a domestic cool box?
  - If yes: Go to Step 5.
  - If no: Place the MP in a suitable cool pack/box. If transporting by vehicle (e.g. home visit), the cool pack/box must be placed in the boot of the car, not on a car seat.

Step 5: Place temperature monitoring device.
  Check: Has a maximum/minimum calibrated temperature probe or Sponsor-approved temperature recording device been placed in the middle of the cool pack/box, with the display panel visible outside?
  - If yes: Go to Step 6.
  - If no: Place the device correctly. It must remain inside the cool pack/box for the entire journey.

Step 6: Check temperature before departure.
  Check: Is the temperature inside the container within the required range specified by the manufacturer/study protocol?
  - If yes: Record the temperature (if required by Sponsor instructions). Go to Step 7.
  - If no: Do NOT proceed with transport. Contact the Clinical Trials Pharmacy Department for advice. END.

Step 7: Transport the MP.
  Check: Minimize the time between removing the MP from cold storage and use. Transport is in progress.
  - Go to Step 8 upon arrival at the destination.

Step 8: Check temperature upon arrival.
  Check: Is the temperature inside the container still within the required range, prior to administering the medication?
  - If yes: Record the temperature (if required by Sponsor instructions). Go to Step 9.
  - If no (temperature has gone outside the required range at any point during transport): Do NOT administer the MP. Contact the Clinical Trials Pharmacy Department for advice on next steps. END.

Step 9: Administer or return the MP.
  Check: Is the MP being dispensed and administered to the participant?
  - If yes: Administer according to the study protocol. END.
  - If no (MP is not dispensed for clinical reasons): Return the MP to the Clinical Trials Pharmacy Department in the cool box with the cold chain maintained if possible. The Pharmacy will store it as quarantined stock. END.
